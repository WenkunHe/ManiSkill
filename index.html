ManiSkill++:
Some Improvements for ManiSkill

Wenkun He*, 1 , Yikai Zheng*, 1 , Yipeng Deng*, 1

1 Tsinghua University

Abstract

We utilize the ManiSkill3 framework to improve upon several classic Reinforcement Learning/Imitative Learning algorithms, which enhances the robot's success rate in task handling to some extent. To test the high scalability and generalizability of reward design techniques in Reinforcement Learning, we designed a series of long-horizon tasks and corresponding multi-stage reward functions, using objects from ManiSkill3 and other items of our own selection, achieving high success rates. Additionally, we analyze the underlying reasons for the varying success rates of different algorithms in handling different tasks, originating from the principles of the algorithms.

5.1 Task Design

Experimental results indicate that in refrigerator-related long-horizon tasks, the gripper cannot reach inside the inner space regardless of its approaching pose. We suspect that there might be an error in the collision mesh detection, so we moved the object placement point to the top of the refrigerator.

5.2 Challenges in Long-horizon Task Design

Coordinating Mobility and Grasping

Mobile manipulator finds it challenging to simultaneously learn upper-body grasping and lower-body movement.

Challenges for Object Release

Mobile manipulator is reluctant to release the object, even after reaching the target position (The white ball in the video).

Ensuring Controlled Object Falldown in Noisy Environments

The determination of feasible object release areas is too lenient, causing the object to slide off from the edges.

Opposite Goals of Opening and Shutting


Due to the opposite reward for opening and closing the drawer, the mobile manipulator is hesitant to fully close it, or the drawer is closed too quickly, causing it to rebound.

Cheating by Bypassing Container Manipulation


TMobile manipulator cheats by directly releasing the object above an open-top drawer or refrigerator, bypassing the process of opening and closing the drawer/door.

Challenges in Rotating Door Closure

Mobile manipulator has difficulty reaching the handle located on the back of the refrigerator door.
